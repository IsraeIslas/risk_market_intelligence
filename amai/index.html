AMAI Socioeconomic Levels (Mexico)

  • A/B: Highest socioeconomic level. Executives, large business owners, and high-level professionals. Strong purchasing power and full access to services, education, and private healthcare.
  • C+: Upper-middle class. Professionals, managers, and small-to-medium business owners. Stable income and strong access to services.
  • C: Middle class. Salaried households with stable income and moderate consumption capacity.
  • D+: Lower-middle class. Limited income growth and partial access to services.
  • D: Low socioeconomic level. Informal employment and limited access to quality services.
  • E: Lowest level. Extreme poverty and high structural vulnerability.
  • N/D: Not determined.
